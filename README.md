# Hospital-Emergency-Room-Analytics-Dashboard      

1. Project Title / Headline
A dynamic and interactive Power BI dashboard designed to monitor emergency room performance—focusing on patient flow, wait times, satisfaction levels, and department referrals to improve operational efficiency and patient care.

2. Short Description / Purpose
This Hospital Emergency Room Dashboard is an interactive Power BI report that helps analyze patient volume, wait times, satisfaction scores, and department referrals. It is designed to support hospital management in monitoring performance, improving patient experience, and optimizing emergency room operations.

3.Tech Stack     
The dashboard was built using the following tools and technologies:
• 📊 Power BI Desktop – Used to create interactive visuals and the overall dashboard layout.
• 📂 Power Query – Used for cleaning, transforming, and preparing hospital data for analysis.
• 🧠 DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – Used to calculate key metrics like patient count, average wait time, and satisfaction score.
• 🧩 Data Modeling – Relationships created between different data fields to enable filtering and accurate insights.
• 🎨 UI/UX Design (Power BI) – Customized layout, colors, and icons to improve readability and user experience.
• 📁 File Formats – .pbix for development and .png for dashboard preview.



4. Features / Highlights       
• Business Problem : Hospital emergency rooms handle a high volume of patients daily, making it difficult to track performance and ensure efficient operations.
Key questions like:         
- How many patients are visiting each month?        
- Are patients being attended on time or delayed?    
- What is the average wait time?    
- Which departments receive the most referrals?   
- How satisfied are patients with the service?     
…are not easy to answer using raw hospital data.    

• Goal of the Dashboard : To build an interactive dashboard that ,Provides a clear overview of emergency room performance , Tracks patient flow, wait times, and satisfaction levels , Helps hospital management improve efficiency and patient care ,Enables quick decision-making using real-time insights.      

• Walkthrough of Key Visuals      

- Key KPIs (Top Section) : Total Number of Patients , Average Wait Time , Patient Satisfaction Score.         
These provide a quick snapshot of hospital performance.      

- Year & Month Filters (Slicers) : Allows filtering by year (2023, 2024) and months. Enables time-based analysis of patient trends

- Patient Attend Status (Pie Chart) : Shows percentage of patients attended on time vs delayed. Helps evaluate operational efficiency

- Gender Analysis (Donut Chart) : Displays distribution of male and female patients. Useful for demographic insights.         

- Admission Status (Table + Bar Indicator) : Shows admitted vs not admitted patients with percentage. Helps understand patient intake and treatment flow.

- Patients by Age Group (Bar Chart) : Displays patient distribution across different age groups. Helps identify which age group visits most frequently.              
 
- Department Referrals (Bar Chart) : Shows number of patients referred to each department (e.g., General Practice, Orthopedics, Cardiology). Helps in resource allocation and department workload analysis.     

• Business Impact & Insights
Operational Efficiency: Identifies delays and helps reduce patient waiting time   
Resource Planning: Helps allocate staff and resources to high-demand departments   
Patient Experience: Tracks satisfaction scores to improve service quality    
Data-Driven Decisions: Enables hospital management to act based on real insights   
Trend Analysis: Helps understand patient patterns across months and years   

Overall, the dashboard transforms hospital data into clear and actionable insights, improving both efficiency and quality of care.

5. Key Findings / Results
The analysis shows that the emergency room handles a high volume of patients, but a noticeable number experience delays, leading to higher wait times and moderate satisfaction levels. Most patients are not admitted, indicating non-critical cases dominate, while certain departments like General Practice and Orthopedics face higher workload. Patient distribution across age groups highlights that adults form the majority, and gender distribution remains balanced. Overall, the insights point towards the need for improving response time, optimizing resource allocation, and enhancing patient experience.
